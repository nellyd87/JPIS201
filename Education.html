Education

Never stop learning.

My education is a result of formal classroom work, on the job training, and real-world work experience. At no point do I believe that anyone has "arrived" or completed their education. Life is an education and I plan to continue to learn as much as possible.

Through my highschool years I recieved my education through homeschooling, idependent study courses, dual enrollment at the local public highschool, and through experiences while living near Washington DC. I've had the unique opportunity to learn through a variety of sources, which prepared me to work hard in college.

Originally intending to graduate in the field of nursing, I attended Utah Valley University directly out of highschool. While completing general education requirements there, I learned of the Recreation Managment program and chose to switch majors and pursue that as a course of study and career. I grew up participating in local events, community programs, etc. and became passionate about the profession when I learned of its opportunities to give back. Upon this discovery, I transferred to BYU for their Recreation Managment program, which specialized in community recreation.

Since working in the field of Parks and Recreation, I have become a member of the National Parks and Recreation Association (NRPA) and earned my Certified Parks and Recreation Professional (CPRP) designation in late 2019. The CPRP designation is one that must be kept current through a committment to continuing education in the profession and identifies those to maintain it as leaders in the industry. I'm proud to be a part of the organization and to work with such a high caliber group ofS CPRP peers.
